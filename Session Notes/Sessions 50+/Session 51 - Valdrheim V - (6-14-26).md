###### Day 32
## Summary
The party fought undead giants and freed Poko Poko, an amusing and arrogant lich who was responsible for bringing Persephone back to life.

## IC Synopsis - Rem's Writings
>I was still a boy the first time I learned of Valdrheim, and I told Ulrich I’d lead the campaign to cleanse the city.  He smiled sadly at me then, the same way he smiled when I asked him about my mother.  Over our years together, I’d learn that he fought in the last of the crusades.  It was not something he enjoyed speaking of, and I knew better than to press him.  Yet all the same I pieced together that it was a disastrous affair, so much so that another crusade had not been organized since.  Only a fool of incomparable hubris could think they would succeed where so many others had failed…
  In the present, we emerged from our temporary shelter to find several undead giants roaming nearby.  Lewis assured us he was battle-ready despite the loss of his hand, which prompted our specter companion to offer their aid.  The ghost took possession of Lewis’s shield and swore their master could provide us succor, if we freed them.  I activated my own shield and Perse shrouded herself in magic as we rushed headlong into another skirmish.  These giants had lost no strength in death, but my cohorts curtailed them with their spellcraft.  A shadow emerged and joined the giants before Valindra Slowed them, while Lewis’s Spirit Guardians and Ria’s Storm Sphere battered them and bent the battlefield.  Perse utilized a Wall of Force to funnel them as we focused down one, then another.  Even then, the giants threw fire and lightning and death at us to great effect.  Lewis unleashed a massive wave of healing when things looked darkest, drawing the ire of our remaining foes upon himself.  When he fell, Perse got him back on his feet with a Healing Word and Wuffi shuttled him to relative safety.  Valindra felled the last of the giants as the ghost urged us westward to a large, scorched building.  Hardly pristine, but still in better shape than the rest of the city.
  Inside was a library of some sort, and the specter bid us onwards into the heart of the structure.  As Lewis and I led our troupe inwards, some of the books sprung to life and flung themselves at us.  Orthodoxy has long maintained that knowledge can be dangerous, but for some reason I don’t think this is quite what the old priests had in mind.  Fortunately for us, the books were a minor inconvenience compared to what we’d faced outside.  In the central chamber, we found a cloaked figure bound within a warded circle.  Valindra and I surveyed the room with enhanced senses, coming to the conclusion that the central circle was some sort of anti-magic trap.  Perse conversed with the creature inside, learning it called itself Poko Poko.  This translates to “Greatest of Great” and their arrogance certainly matched their name.
  Yet for all their pompousness, Poko Poko was quite forthcoming with information.  They had inadvertently trapped themselves while conducting research in Valdrheim, and had attempted numerous contingency plans to facilitate their escape.  Persephone was apparently Plan C, though they had been trapped long enough to get at least as far as Plan G.  Poko Poko originally hailed from Tabenak, the geographically and culturally isolated Southwestern corner of Skediwen.  They mentioned self-improvement as a goal for their visit, but went on to elaborate when I pressed for more information.  Poko Poko believes Valdrheim to be a font of Negative Energy, a well that burst when the local king cursed the Rexum for slaughtering Valdrheim’s residents following a failed rebellion forty years past.  My thoughts turned to The Living God’s speech on the Grand Divine and the current cosmology of our world, something they likened to a page ripped from a book.  Was this Negative Energy well, as Poko Poko described it, a symptom of this celestial arrangement?  A question for Hypithia, but I dared not reveal her in front of Poko Poko.
  Persephone continued her interrogation, with myself and the others contributing an occasional query.  Valindra learned the local dragon could hardly be described as such due to the corruption it had experienced, and my mention of Hypithia drew disparaging comments from our captive.  Perse negotiated for Poko Poko’s assistance against the Rexum as well as in Valdrheim.  The would-be overlord of Valdrheim readily agreed to most of our demands, but would not go so far as to swear fealty to our new regime.  Satisfied with our arrangement, Valindra and I moved one of the statues powering the prison and freed Poko Poko.  Persephone asked after Zagreus then, learning her son is being held against his will and that he has attempted to find her himself, though his own means are limited.  The enigmatic undead creature then placed something against a door and cast a spell, ushering us all into Poko Poko’s Prodigious Palace. 
  The spellcraft was stunning, but the sense of relief was greater yet.  We’d survived our first day in this damned city, even if it cost Lewis a hand.  A terrible loss to be sure, but as my thoughts drifted once more to Ulrich and his failed crusade, I knew it could have been much, much worse.

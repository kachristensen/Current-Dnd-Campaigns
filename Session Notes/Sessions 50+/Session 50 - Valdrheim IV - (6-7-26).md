###### Day 32
## Summary
The party continued to fight undead before following a ghost who requested they come help its trapped master, who knew Persephone.

## IC Synopsis - Frey
>With another wave of undead nipping at our heels, we pressed onward along the northward route through Valdrheim. We made little progress before we were stalled by another encounter, although at least this time we were able to somewhat position ourselves beforehand, for lack of yet another shrieking undead dryad. Even so, forward progress was stalled while this next battle ensued. Valindra in particular was badly wounded, Wuffi destroyed and resummoned, and Lewis lost a hand to a bundle of skulls attached to fleshy wings. I would say we are fortunate there were no permanent deaths to report in this instance. 
  (Note to self: Engage any future encountered Angatra with extreme prejudice.)
  Following a brief pause to collect ourselves and recover via Divine Intervention (Prayer of Healing cast, sped up), we continued north. A spirit of some nature was observed watching us at a juncture along our way. As it was not immediately hostile, the group elected to converse with it. 
  In particular, the spirit called to Persephone by name, implying some knowledge of her, and further informing us that its master had long awaited her arrival. It warned us against continuing along our northerly path, instead begging us to accompany it to meet its master, the name of whom it was seemingly unable to provide, along with most other details aside from the fact that this master supposedly had some power over undeath.
  We elected to follow it, diverting toward the west. After some deliberation, held under the cover of a building the spirit was able to temporarily ward, we elected to satisfy the spirit and meet its master (and owner of its soul, we learned). The spirit took shelter in the body of Persephone’s moth companion, for want of volunteers elsewise, and we set out once more, soon encountering a band of undead giants along our new path.

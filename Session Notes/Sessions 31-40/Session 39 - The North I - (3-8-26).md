## Summary

## IC Synopsis - Lewis
>We found the body of a fallen frost giant along the road, and within its chest Rem discovered something strange— a carved totem where the giant’s heart should have been. The runes etched into it were familiar to me, the same markings Rhenka had once shown me. When I studied them, their meaning became clear. “Second life.”  Valindra sensed a trace of Fey magic within it, though none of us understood its true purpose. Rem kept the totem with him as we continued on.  A few hours later we arrived at Rhenka’s village. The camp was small and quiet, guarded by little more than a circle of tents and a young white dragon chained to a post. Rhenka called out, and several giants emerged— mostly children. Soon after, she returned with her father, Chief Farbauti, and his wife Snjor. The chief thanked us for bringing his daughter home but made sure to shame her for allowing herself to be captured.  Talk soon turned to darker matters. The giants spoke of their dwindling numbers and their ongoing war with the snow elves, whose attacks had nearly wiped out Rhenka’s clan. Rem showed the chief the strange totem we had found, and Farbauti confirmed the feud still burned strong between their peoples.  To celebrate Rhenka’s return, the giants prepared a feast. I offered them the dragon meat we carried, which delighted them enough that they tossed me around the camp like some sort of festival toy. Apparently drunken clerics make excellent entertainment.  As the night went on, conversations deepened. Persephone spoke with Rem about his past and the possibility of him one day leading Skediwen. Rem admitted he feared the corruption that often follows power and said he would only accept such a crown if his mentor Ulrich approved it.  Elsewhere, Rhenka slipped away from the celebration. Ria followed her and coaxed out a painful truth— Rhenka lives beneath the shadow of her father and the memory of a sister once meant to inherit the clan.
  Worse still, Rhenka confessed that her sister yet lives… and that one day she may have to kill her.  By that point I had drunk myself senseless and passed out beneath the tents.  Later in the night I stepped outside the camp and reached out through magic to the Living God, asking if they still survived despite their sister’s pursuit. I told them I needed their strength to break Rem’s curse. They answered not with words but with a dream.  In that dream we spoke plainly. The Living God confirmed that Rem’s lycanthropy is not natural— demonic corruption has twisted it. A simple Remove Curse would not save him. I proposed another solution: drain all of Rem’s blood and replace it, purging the corruption entirely. The Living God believed it could work, suggesting we might replace his blood with enchanted sap… or even our own if we were willing.  Before the dream ended, the Living God left me with another name.  Saint Imelda. She still hears prayers, they said— but she is trapped.  Morning came, and the giants fed us before we continued north. Chief Farbauti spoke of the leader behind the snow elves’ recent attacks— a woman known only as the Matron, who has united the tribes against the giants. Rem suggested we pursue the elves ourselves before drawing the giants fully into battle.
  Snjor also mentioned rumors of a tribe to the east capable of shapeshifting, perhaps connected to Rem’s curse.  While we prepared to leave, Persephone uncovered painful truths of her own. Her son Zagreus had spent years trying to bring her back after her death. When even Hades could not revive her, Zagreus turned his anger against him. Since then, father and son have been locked in conflict in the realm of Xchimaltia, where Hades now exists in a strange state between life and death.  Rem swore he would help Persephone find her son one day.  Not long after leaving the giants’ camp, Rhenka slipped away from her family and joined us on the road. Before long we encountered a hag, who warned us against traveling further north. Persephone answered her with a threat, and the creature vanished into the cold.  So we pressed onward.  The full moon was only three days away, Rem’s curse still lingered, and somewhere in the north a Matron of snow elves prepared for war.

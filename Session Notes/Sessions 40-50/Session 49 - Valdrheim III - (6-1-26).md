## IC Synopsis - Lewis
>The battle began with Persephone launching a devastating mind sliver into the enemy ranks, opening the fight with a surge of psychic pain. Valindra immediately charged the Dryad, her blades striking twice and carving deep wounds into the creature. I called forth my Spirit Guardians, sending divine spirits swirling around me that tore into two enemies as they advanced. Before we could press our advantage, more foes emerged from the ruins. One cloaked in a dark aura struck Valindra with tremendous force, while Ria unleashed Wuffie's hidden draconic power, bathing the battlefield in dragon breath before following it with a powerful blast of lightning that shattered another foe. Amid the chaos, Rem surged forward and cut down the Dryad, but the victory was short-lived as even more zombies clawed their way from the rubble. Greater zombies rushed our position, and one landed a brutal critical blow against Rem. Somehow, he shrugged it off without serious injury and kept fighting. Persephone called out to the Blighted Acolyte, demanding its attention, but the creature barely acknowledged her. Valindra pressed her attack but failed to land a blow, while I raced through the battlefield, dragging my Spirit Guardians through as many undead as possible and burning them with divine radiance.
  Then the battle turned. The Blighted Acolyte stepped forward and twisted reality itself, casting Reverse Gravity. In an instant, Ria, myself, and countless zombies were hurled a hundred feet into the air. For a moment there was only panic and open sky. Ria reacted immediately, summoning her electric dragon and skillfully maneuvering it beneath me as I fell. At the same time, Rem charged through the battlefield toward the Acolyte and struck true, breaking the spell. The zombies plummeted to the earth and crashed into the ground below. I fell as well, desperately reaching for salvation, and managed to grab hold of the dragon's tail. Hanging on for dear life, I eventually pulled myself onto its back and thanked Ria for her quick thinking. Below us, a Greater Zombie continued its assault on Valindra while Persephone shouted commands across the battlefield and conjured a magical blade, only to have the Acolyte negate the spell before it could strike. Valindra responded with a devastating barrage that struck four enemies at once, cutting a path through the horde.
  From atop the dragon, Ria carried me over the battlefield while my Spirit Guardians washed over the undead below, burning them wherever we passed. Rem skillfully maneuvered Annica into a favorable position within the reach of my guardians, and together we pressed the attack. When a Soul Shaker emerged from the horde, I called upon divine power and smote the creature into ruin. At that moment, the Executioner entered the battle, a towering monster that immediately struck at Ria's dragon while the Blighted Acolyte unleashed a Harm spell that tore through me with necrotic force. Ria retaliated with a powerful orb of energy that exploded against our enemies before steering the dragon back toward Valindra's position to support the front line. Rem struggled against a poison coursing through his body, failing to purge it despite his efforts, yet he continued fighting and regrouped with the rest of us. Around us, the zombies swung wildly but failed to break our defenses.
  Sensing an opportunity, Persephone commanded spectral hands to release Valindra and summoned her spider companion, which ensnared both the Executioner and the Blighted Acolyte, pinning them in place. Valindra seized the opening and landed a series of powerful strikes that left the enemies reeling. Then Ria delivered the decisive blow, her dragon tearing into the Blighted Acolyte and ending its threat once and for all. Rem destroyed the Soul Shaker soon after, and the tiny grasping hands that emerged from its corpse were instantly consumed by my Spirit Guardians. I attempted to cleanse the poison from Rem but failed, while Ria continued hammering our enemies with lightning. Eventually, Rem managed to purge the poison himself and returned to full fighting strength. Over the next several moments, we focused everything we had on the Executioner. Using flyby tactics from the dragon and coordinated attacks from every member of the party, we slowly wore the monster down until it finally fell beneath our combined assault. Exhausted but victorious, we barely had time to catch our breath before the ground around us began to stir once more. Fresh enemies started clawing their way out of the earth, threatening to overwhelm us through sheer numbers.
  We pressed north into the ruined town, determined to seize the momentum we had fought so hard to earn. Around us lay the broken remains of the undead horde, the Blighted Acolyte, and the Executioner, but there was no time to celebrate. The dead were still rising from the earth, clawing their way from shattered streets and collapsed buildings. Bloodied, exhausted, and running low on strength, we tightened our formation and advanced deeper into the town. Every alley seemed to hide another pack of undead, every ruined structure another lurking horror. Yet none of us faltered. We had survived the worst the enemy could throw at us, and now it was our turn to bring the fight to them. With steel, spell, lightning, and divine wrath, we pushed forward through the darkness, cutting down every undead creature foolish enough to stand in our path. The battle behind us had been won, but ahead lay the heart of the corruption, and we intended to carve our way straight through it.

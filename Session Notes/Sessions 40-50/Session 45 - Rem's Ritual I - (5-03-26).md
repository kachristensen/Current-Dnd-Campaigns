###### Day 28-29
## Summary
The party left the ice cave and returned to the yeti cave, then started the ritual to purge Rem of his affliction.

## IC Synopsis - Ria
>Chaos unfolded in the chambers beyond where we fought the Matron, although we elected not to investigate, lest we risk further, unnecessary battles. I can only assume that without the Matron to keep them in check, the undead and fey ran rampant against the snow elves. This provided us ample cover to recover her head, the head of Rhenka’s fallen sister, and some other spoils of our victory, listed below: 
	- 4600gp
    - Cloak of Elven Kind
    - Eldritch Staff
  We retreated back the way we’d come, healed and roused the Korreds spared earlier and began to dig through the ice covering our exit. It is unlikely the remaining fey will prove as troublesome as the snow elves for the Frost Giants, if for nothing else than their own preservation. Potential alliances are yet to be explored, although the seeds have been planted by Persephone. After escaping the glacial caverns, we returned to the yeti cave to shelter for the night, and determine plans for the cleansing ritual for Rembrandt on the morrow. After some discussion it was determined the ritual would be conducted inside the cave, during which Rembrandt would undergo exsanguination, and receive replacement blood from the party as part of his resurrection. All present were deemed acceptable donors.
  No noteworthy disturbances occurred during the night’s watch. Porto arrived at some point, reportedly to observe and return to its master with news of the ritual’s results. Prior to this, Rhenka expressed some concern regarding my mental state, as she herself had found the results of vengeance to be unfulfilling. I made my best effort to offer her reassurances, although I suspect she finds my deflections unconvincing. There is little for it, alas. In the morning, Lewis handed out silvered weapons to the party in the event of necessary violence, and set about preparing for the ritual. He laid out a circle for the magic, and crafted silvered chains, while Persephone cast another dome which Rem would not be able to enter. We were left with two last requests from Rembrandt in the event of his death: ensure the god-fruit is used to end the Secomortis plague, and to spread his ashes near Shadeleaf, his home. Before allowing himself to be bound within the ritual circle, he cast Aid on the rest of the party, and cast Daylight on a locket, which he removed and placed on the ground before him. The ritual commenced. Lewis placed the spell Heat Metal on the chains while we gathered within Persephone’s Tiny Hut. Here, I think, was the last point at which the ritual went to plan. Rem’s transformation was more gradual than expected, prompting Lewis to hesitate and Persephone to step in and cut Rembrandt’s throat herself.
  However, rather than simply pool, the blood separated into two distinct masses, one nearly pitch-dark, the other shimmering strangely. The former mass shaped itself into a demonic form, with mixed canine and arachnid features, while the latter became a fleshy, winged thing with many mouths. While Rem lay bleeding, the rest of us engaged in combat with these two creatures.

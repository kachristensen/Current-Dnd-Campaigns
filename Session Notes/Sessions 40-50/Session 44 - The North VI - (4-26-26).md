###### Day 28
## Summary
The party slew the matron and her creatures.

## IC Synopsis - Lewis
>We entered as one, but it was Persephone and her familiars who took the front, spilling into the room like a living shield. I barely had time to take in the space before bolts cut through the air—one striking the lion, the next dropping it into an unnatural sleep. Valindra didn’t hesitate, already chasing the source of the shots, while I called upon my faith and summoned my spirit guardians, their radiant forms circling me in a protective storm. Persephone’s creatures surged forward together, a coordinated assault, and Rhenka’s axe flew with a wet squish as it struck true. Rem and Annica rushed a pot-bellied foe and brought it down hard, while Valindra faltered for a moment before landing a solid hit against a drow. Something moved in the shadows, and I stepped forward into it, my guardians tearing into both the Ice Maiden and her ally. Persephone’s voice rose over the chaos, commanding the battlefield itself, while Rhenka charged with a blow that should have crushed bone—but the Ice Maiden barely reacted. Then Ria answered with fire, her sorcery erupting in flames that the creature clearly feared. In a blink, the Ice Maiden teleported, killing Persephone’s spider, but Rem struck her in return, and Valindra stepped in to deliver the killing blow. There was barely a pause. Valindra spoke in Sylvan, and from the fog the hag answered, claiming she would talk—if we would. I stepped forward, demanding she drop the fog, but she refused. That was answer enough. I pressed on, my spirit guardians striking her as the rest of the party rushed in. We overwhelmed her quickly, knocking her unconscious—only for Persephone to end it without hesitation. We took a breath, checked wounds, and pushed forward… only to be met with a deafening roar that shook the halls.
  Rem was the first to answer it, his strike landing with devastating precision as radiant power flared from his blade. But the creature responded with terrifying speed—webbing burst across the battlefield, binding many of us where we stood. I was caught fast, unable to move, as Persephone disrupted the matron and a storm of ice crashed down on her and Rem—both resisting its fury. Valindra endured another storm, Rhenka remained trapped, and Ria blinked free of the webbing, summoning a dragon into the fight. Then the monstrosity revealed itself, its roar pressing fear into the room, shaking even Ria and Valindra. Rem answered with defiance, bone-like wings tearing from his back as he broke free. Lightning cracked from Valindra’s spear, and Persephone tried to seize control again—only to be countered, her form twisted into that of a penguin. Ria reacted instantly, her fireball breaking the spell as her draconic spirit struck with brutal force. Still bound, I fought against the webbing until—by sheer will and a fortunate turn—I tore myself free. Silence fell over part of the battlefield, muting Ria, Rem, and Persephone, but they pressed on regardless. Rem healed Persephone and struck again, Valindra traded blows with the matron, and Ria—restrained, frightened, and silenced—still unleashed lightning through sheer determination as her dragon continued its assault. I finally closed the distance, my spirit guardians cutting into the enemy once more—but the monstrosity answered with a sweeping blow that shattered them and nearly dropped me where I stood. Then came the cold. A blast of freezing death that cut through all of us—Valindra falling as the rest of us staggered, barely holding on.
  But we did not break. Ria found her moment and drove a sorcerous burst straight through the matron’s chest, ending her. I turned then, focusing everything I had left—not on survival, but on ending it. Calling on what strength remained, I stepped forward and brought my mace down against the monstrosity, finishing it at last. The chamber fell still. We gathered ourselves, breath ragged, wounds fresh, and prepared to move—but even as we steadied, the sound of steel and roaring battle echoed from the corridors ahead, reminding us this fight was far from over.
